## The Problem with Centralised Systems

In today's world, centralised systems dominate our communities and governance structures, stifling innovation and individual empowerment. Unequal resource distribution further exacerbates societal divides, while rigid hierarchies limit creativity and adaptability. Moreover, centralised systems are vulnerable to single points of failure, jeopardising the entire community's stability. The result is a world where individual potential remains largely untapped, and collective progress is hindered by outdated, inefficient structures.
